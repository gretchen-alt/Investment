Why rotation, not reaction

Rules before emotion

Every rotation in this portfolio is pre-committed before the volatility that triggers it arrives. That's the point — the decision gets made in a calm moment, not a frightened one.

①

Laddered entries, never momentum chases

Limit-order ladders replace the anxious, reactive buy. When ONTO and BESI ran far past sane entry points this June, the answer was to set lower rungs and wait — not to chase the print.

②

Macro triggers, not macro guesses

Dry powder stays parked until the Fed has cut three or more times, the dollar index has trended down for a month, the Sahm rule has normalized, and the 10s2s spread turns positive. All four, not a hunch about one.

③

Twelve months as a circuit breaker

The long-term capital gains floor isn't just a tax mechanic — it's a forced cooling-off period that keeps a bad week from becoming a bad decision.

④

No duplicated theses

AEM stays off the core sleeve while FNV and WPM already cover the royalty angle on gold. SLV earns its place as a deliberate, non-royalty exception — not an accident of overlap.
